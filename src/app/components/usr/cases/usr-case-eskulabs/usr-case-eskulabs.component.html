Non-invasive diagnosis

Definition

Non-invasive diagnostics - research or treatment, during which the skin is not affected by needles or various surgical instruments (Ultrasound and other devices based on the use of sensor and signal transmitting devices)

The risks
Interpretation of data from survey images

Often there are times when several specialists give out several different interpretations and descriptions for the same ultrasound images.

Error in the use of medical equipment

For example, depending on the correctness of the determination by the ultrasound operator of the apparatus of the boundaries of the tumor (determined manually), the calculation of the size of the tumor may be erroneous. As a result of an incorrect calculation of the tumor size, the diagnosis may be different.

Decision

Creation of an information platform for the quality control of non-invasive medical diagnostics

SoftData Approach
Step 1. Pre-filter data using information systems

Comparison of the doctor’s examination protocol with the examination results carried out by the Esculabs information system based on neural networks

Step 2. Expert opinion

In case of discrepancy between the results of the doctor’s examination and the SoftData information system, appeal to experts for a third-party opinion (any expert authorized and having access to the system can give a conclusion anywhere in the world.

Research on liver fibrosis

The data from fibroscans in 4 hepatocenters in the Republic of Kazakhstan for the period September 2013 - July 2015 were analyzed.

An average of 30% of incorrect examinations was revealed as a result of not following the inspection regulations and making errors when using medical equipment
